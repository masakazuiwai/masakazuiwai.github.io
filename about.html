  • All lead to Photosynthesis

    Did you know we can enjoy a cup of coffee every morning thanks to Photosynthesis? Coffee beans are from flowering plants (Genus Coffea). Roasting beans requires heat using fire, which is a reaction between oxygen and fuel. Oxygen in the air is produced by photosynthesis. Fuel also originated from decomposed plant materials. If you like to add some milk, that is from cows, which are dependent on plants. If you need some sugar, that's also from plants - sugar cane. No photosynthesis, no coffee.

  • Let's talk about my research

    This animated image shows a false-colored fluorescence image of a chloroplast observed from the top to bottom by using a super-resolution microscope. The size is about 8 µm (8/1000 mm) in diameter. Each of the circular structures is granum - a part of photosynthetic membranes, where water is split, and oxygen is generated through photosynthetic reactions. Within each granum, numerous photosynthetic proteins exist to regulate the photosynthetic reactions. My research aims to understand how light energy triggers photosynthetic mechanisms and how photosynthetic proteins and membranes have an impact on the regulations.

  • About NIC

    When I was a high school student in Nara, Japan, I didn't see a point to go to college. So, I was thinking maybe I wanna be working at a flower shop, instead, because it would be fun to take care of flowers and plants every day (didn't think through how hard it would be, though...). But, life is interesting - there were many turning points in the last summer of high school, which made me decide to go abroad and study environmental sciences in the US. To make that happen, I moved to Tokyo by myself, worked as a newspaper boy, and studied English at "NIC International College in Japan." I went there because I needed a TOEFL score to get into a college in the US. At NIC, I learned not only English but also how fun it is to learn something new when you have a real purpose for learning. If there weren't NIC, I wouldn't be here, researching photosynthesis at LBNL.
